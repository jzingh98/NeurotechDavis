ABOUT US

Neurotech@Davis is a student-led organization at the University of California, Davis whose mission is to facilitate the advancement and awareness of neurotechnology by providing undergraduates with the opportunity to foster skills in this industry. We aim to accomplish this through BCI projects, collaborative work with neuroscience faculty, neurotech events, and more!

Overview

Neurotechnology broadly refers to any system that establishes a direct connection between the nervous system and an external electronic device. Neural Interfaces, also called Brain Computer Interfaces (BCIs), have been used for applications ranging from prosthetics to thought based control of drones. Ongoing research efforts are underway at companies and university labs across the world to explore further applications of this technology.

Club Organization

Neurotech@Davis is an affiliate member of NeurotechX and the NeurotechX Student Clubs Initiative. This is a global organization that connects neurotechnology researchers and enthusiasts from across the world to promote innovation in the field. Neurotech@Davis represents the University of California, Davis to this global community. We have ongoing collaborations with other NeurotechX affiliate clubs, including CRUX at UCLA, and we hope to connect with members from other universities to exchange ideas and work together on projects. NeurotechX also organizes an annual compeition which all affiliate members may participate in.

Our club consists of several division, each working separately to advance our mission. The Projects group is responsible for designing and engineering exciting new neural interface projects to demonstrate the capabilities of this technology, while providing a learning opportunity to students. The Research group provides high quality content to our community exploring the latest topics in neurotechnology, computational neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and neuroethics. Our Outreach group organizes events and maintains relationships to promote our organization, and neurotechnology at large, within our community

Membership

Neurotech@Davis is composed of a diverse pool of members coming from backgrounds in computer science, cognitive science, electrical engineering, neuroscience, and biomedical engineering. We welcome everyone to attend our general meetings. Individuals who are willing and able to commit 4 hours per week or more are invited to apply to join one of our core divisions. These include the Projects Division and the Research Division detailed below.

Club Divisions

In addition to general meetings and activities which are open to the entire UC Davis community, Neurotech@Davis includes two club divisions which operate as self contained units within the organization to advance our vision. Membership into a division requires additional time commitment and is contingent on need and relevant skills.

  • Projects Division

    Neurotech@Davis plans to carry out several projects throughout the school year to provide a hands on learning experience for students, and to demonstrate existing applications of neural interfaces. We also plan to participate in the NeurotechX Brain Hack competition.
    The projects division offers excellent opportunities to gain hand on experience developing brain-computer interfaces. Each project allows members to develop skills in various hardware and software disciples, as well as basic neurobiology.

  • Research Division

    At Neurotech@Davis, we aim to highlight the most relevant breakthroughs and projects in neuroscience, computer science, and the neurotechnology industry as a whole! Check out our Medium blog posts for more information on the latest studies and products in neurotechnology. Contact mpitkin@ucdavis.edu for inquiries.
    This is an excellent opportunity to develop skills researching and writing high quality content on complex, cutting edge topics aimed to educate the public.
